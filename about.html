About Me

Hi, I’m Monika!

I’m actively seeking entry-level IT roles such as IT Specialist, Help Desk, Desktop Support, and I’m open to exploring other opportunities where I can grow and contribute. My long-term goal is to become an IT project manager, and I’m currently focused on strengthening my technical foundation beyond what I’ve gained through education and project work.

Growing up, my family moved every three years because my dad served in the Navy. I’ve lived in places like Bahrain and Japan, which gave me a broader perspective and a love for experiencing different cultures. I’m Black and Japanese and attended a Japanese school on weekends, so I’m fluent in Japanese. In my free time, I stay active with running, enjoy cooking, and love going to concerts because live music is one of my favorite things.

My Experience

I earned my Master’s in Management in Spring 2025 and my Bachelor’s in IT with a cybersecurity concentration in Spring 2024, both from George Mason University. My coursework included programming, information and network security, operating systems, and database fundamentals, complemented by project-based experience. I’ve gained experience working within technical, local government, and educational environments, and I hold the CompTIA Security+ certification.

I’m actively involved with the National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE), which supports the academic and professional growth of Black engineers. At GMU, I served as Telecommunications Chair and President of our NSBE chapter. I’m also a member of the Louis Stokes Alliance for Minority Participation (LSAMP), a program dedicated to increasing diversity in STEM. Through LSAMP, I served as a peer mentor and point of contact, offering support to undergraduates pursuing STEM degrees at Mason.
